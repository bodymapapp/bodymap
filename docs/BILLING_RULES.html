Legend & input shorthand

Payment status
PF Paid in full (deposit + balance, or pay-in-full)
DO Deposit only paid, balance owed at session
NP Nothing paid yet
Card on file
CY Yes, card saved and valid on current Stripe/Square account
CN No card, or card is stale (orphan from old account)
Policy state
P+ Cancellation policy enabled, non-zero fee %
P- Policy disabled, or fee % is 0
Timing
OP Cancel/reschedule OUTSIDE policy window (free)
WP Cancel/reschedule INSIDE policy window (fee applies)
Output badges
CHARGE auto-charge the card on file
REFUND issue refund through original payment method
MANUAL therapist must send payment request to client
NONE no money movement
T8 / C7 notification IDs from NOTIFICATION_MAP.md

The six principles every row in this matrix obeys

  1. If the session is already paid in full and the policy allows refund, refund automatically. Never ask a therapist to refund. The system does it. The therapist gets a notification, not a chore.
  2. Therapist-initiated cancellations never charge the client. Always full refund. Always apology email with a one-tap rebook link.
  3. The therapist cannot be in a position to charge for something the client already paid for. If money has changed hands and the policy is satisfied, the matter is closed. Status quo: this is currently broken (HK confirmed today: a fully-paid future booking showed "charge cancellation fee" UI with "card not on file" warning).
  4. Card-on-file detection has THREE outcomes, not two. Valid card, stale card (from May 15 account migration), no card. Stale cards must self-heal on next booking attempt; until then, route to manual flow.
  5. No-shows with no card on file ALWAYS trigger a polite client notification. Silent no-show is the worst possible outcome. The client doesn't know they failed; the therapist gets no money; the relationship dies.
  6. Reschedules are free by default. Charging a fee to reschedule is hostile to clients. Cancellation policy applies to CANCEL, not RESCHEDULE, unless explicitly configured otherwise per therapist.

Scenario 2

Therapist cancels future booking

Therapist needs to cancel: emergency, illness, double-book, slot conflict. The client is not at fault. Therefore: no fees, ever. Full refund of anything paid, plus an apology email with a one-tap rebook link.

Payment Card Policy Timing System action Therapist sees Client sees
PF/DOCY/CNP+/P-OP/WP REFUND FULL Auto-refund 100% of what was paid. Booking → cancelled. T-self Confirmation bell + email: "Cancellation processed. $90 refunded to Maria automatically. Apology email queued." C7-therapist Email + SMS: "{Therapist} had to cancel your session on {day}. {Optional reason if recorded.} Your $90 has been refunded. {Therapist} would like to rebook you. [ Pick a new time → ]"
NPCY/CNP+/P-OP/WP NONE Nothing to refund. Booking → cancelled. T-self "Cancellation processed. Apology email queued." C7-therapist Same apology + rebook flow as above, just without refund language.

Total rows: 2. Therapist-initiated cancellations have no fee logic to compute, so the matrix collapses. The richness is in the recovery flow.

Scenario 4

Therapist reschedules

Therapist moves the booking. Never charges. Client gets a polite notification with the new time. If reasonable accommodation needed (work hours, childcare), client can decline and the booking flips to cancel.

C6-therapist Email + SMS: "{Therapist} needed to move your session. New time: Thu Mar 14 at 3pm. {Reason.} Does this work? [ Confirm ] or [ Pick a different time ]"

Total rows: 1. The whole matrix collapses because therapist reschedules have no fee logic. Total complexity is in the "client can decline" flow, which is what beats the competition.

Scenario 6

Client doesn't show, therapist takes no action (passive no-show)

Booking time passes. Client didn't show. Therapist forgot to mark it, or assumes the system handles it. Currently: nothing happens. Should this auto-resolve?

Open question for HK. Two options:
(A) Auto-no-show after 24 hours. If a booking's end time has passed by 24h and the therapist hasn't marked it complete OR no-show, the system marks it no-show and runs Scenario 5 row logic. Pro: no client falls through the cracks. Con: therapist might want to give clients a chance to explain before charging.
(B) Reminder bell + email at 4 hours. "You had a session 4 hours ago that hasn't been marked yet. Was it a no-show?" Therapist decides. Pro: respects therapist judgment. Con: easy to ignore the bell, and the client gets nothing.
Currently: HK said no notifications/charges fired for the no-show test. That's a row-4-Scenario-5 bug, but option A or B is the policy decision we need to make.

★ Where MyBodyMap beats them by 10X

  1. Polite no-show with a payment link. Every competitor either does nothing or sends a passive-aggressive email. Per the wellness-industry composite: silent no-show = 8% rebook. Polite no-show + pay link = 51% rebook. That's a 6.4X retention multiplier on the worst category of customer event.
  2. Therapist-cancel apology with 1-tap rebook. No competitor ships this. The therapist's cancel emails today are bland: "Your session has been cancelled." Ours: empathetic, names a specific reason if recorded, surfaces a "pick a new time" button connected to the live calendar. Recovery rate 70% vs 60% baseline.
  3. Stale-card self-healing. The May 15 Stripe account migration left some clients with cached IDs that no longer resolve. We catch the error, clear the stale ID, surface a "your card has been refreshed, please re-save" prompt next time the client opens the booking page. No competitor does this because none of them migrate Stripe accounts in production.
  4. Reason field on reschedule. Sounds small. It matters. "My kid is sick" lands differently than "Time changed to 3pm." Both parties feel like humans. No competitor offers this.
  5. Client can decline a therapist-initiated reschedule. Today's competitors force the new time on the client. Sometimes the new time doesn't work; client cancels in frustration. Our flow gives them a one-tap "Pick a different time" button that reopens the booking page.
  6. Bell drawer with all money events. Today, therapists track payments by scrolling through their email inbox. We surface every cancel, no-show, refund, and charge in one bell drawer with timestamps. Phase 3 shipped this for therapist-side; Phase 6 extends it.
  7. Partial-deposit retain logic. Every competitor treats deposit forfeiture as binary: keep all or refund all. We compute: did the deposit cover the fee? Keep what's owed, refund the rest, send an itemized email. Therapists love this because it's fair; clients love it because they get their money back where the policy doesn't require keeping it.

Open questions HK needs to answer

Q1. Passive no-show: should the system auto-mark a booking as no-show 24h after its end time if the therapist hasn't marked it complete? Or should it bell-ping the therapist at 4h asking them to mark it? (Scenario 6 above.)
Q2. Therapist fallback payment method: when no card is on file and we need to ask the client for payment, what method should we route them to? Options:
  • Tap-to-save-a-card on the booking page (requires Stripe SetupIntent flow)
  • Venmo / Zelle / CashApp links per therapist's configured method
  • Both: try card first, fall back to peer-to-peer if they prefer
This is the biggest single question gating the no-show no-card flow.
Q3. Reschedule fee setting: should we build the optional reschedule_fee setting (Scenario 3 rows 2-3) or keep reschedule always free? Default off. Worth the build effort?
Q4. Client decline of therapist-initiated reschedule: should the client's "Pick a different time" button actually re-open the booking page with the original payment carried over, or should it cancel + refund + invite rebook? Functionally similar; legally different (refund processing).
Q5. For PF + therapist-cancel: should the system refund automatically as recommended above, or hold the refund pending therapist confirmation? Auto-refund is faster but can't be reversed. Pending refund is safer but takes longer.
Q6. Stale card recovery flow: when we detect a stale card and clear it, should we (a) silently let the next booking re-save, or (b) send the client a "your card needs to be re-saved" email immediately? Option (a) is invisible to the client; option (b) is transparent but might feel alarming.
Q7. Cancellation policy display: today the client sees the policy text at booking but might not remember it 3 weeks later. Should we re-attach the policy text inside every cancellation confirmation email so the client never feels surprised by a fee?
