Use whichever payment processor you already use. If you do not use either yet, start with Stripe. The two are equally supported on every online feature MyBodyMap offers.

## What both do equally well

- Online deposits at booking
- Package and gift certificate purchases
- Card on file for cancellation policy
- Automatic charge on late cancel or no-show
- One-tap refunds from your dashboard

## Where they differ

**Stripe auto-renews monthly memberships.** If you sell recurring monthly memberships ("unlimited massage for one hundred fifty per month"), Stripe charges the card automatically every month. Hands-off.

**Square asks you to nudge each renewal.** Square's recurring billing requires you to confirm each renewal manually from your dashboard. One tap per month per member, but it is one tap. If you sell a lot of memberships, Stripe is smoother. If you only sell occasional memberships or none at all, Square is fine.

**Square has slightly narrower browser support.** About five percent of clients on older Safari may not be able to save a card on file with Square. Stripe works on more browsers. If you have older clients on older devices and you require card-on-file at booking, Stripe handles them better.

## Why you might use both

If you connect both, MyBodyMap routes each feature to whichever processor handles it best. Settings, Payments, then Payment routing. You can override per feature: memberships through Stripe, card-on-file through Square, deposits through whichever you prefer.

Most therapists pick one and stick with it. Connecting both is for therapists who already have established relationships with both companies.

## Setup time

Stripe takes about five minutes. They walk you through identity verification and bank account linking.

Square takes longer the first time because of their merchant activation step. Plan for ten to fifteen minutes. If you already use Square in person, your account may already be activated.

## A common question: my bank says they offer payment processing too

Most banks offer "online payments" that are actually Stripe, Adyen, or another processor white-labeled with your bank's logo. Same fees, sometimes plus a small markup the bank keeps. We support Stripe and Square directly which gives you the same thing without the bank in between.

A small number of banks offer real ACH bank-to-bank payment options, which are different. Lower fees but slower settlement. We are looking at adding ACH as an option in the future, not yet.
