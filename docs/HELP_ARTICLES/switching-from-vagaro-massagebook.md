Most of our therapists came from Vagaro or MassageBook. The honest comparison.

## The short version

- **Vagaro** is built for spas with five chairs and ten staff. You are one therapist.
- **MassageBook** was built in 2013 and still looks like it. They paywall card-on-file.
- **ClinicSense** is built for therapists who bill insurance. If you are cash-pay, you are paying for clinical features you do not need.
- **Acuity** is general booking software. It does not understand massage therapy.
- **GlossGenius** is built for beauty professionals. Most therapists are not beauty professionals.
- **Jane App** is excellent for clinics but expensive ($74-$109/month) for solo cash-pay.
- **Paper or Google Calendar** is free, and we get it. But paper does not text reminders, charge for no-shows, or notice when a client has not been in for three months.

## Specific differences from Vagaro

- Vagaro takes 12 steps to set up a calendar. We take 3.
- Vagaro Pay is Stripe under the hood with their markup. Same fees, different invoice.
- Vagaro mobile UX is slow even though they market it as mobile-first.
- We have body-map intake. They have text-only intake.
- We have longitudinal pattern detection. They have basic notes.

## Specific differences from MassageBook

- MassageBook paywalls card-on-file at their higher tier. We include it on Bronze (free).
- MassageBook UI has not been substantially updated in years.
- MassageBook advertised price is $25/month. Real cost with the features you need is $70-$100. Our advertised Silver price is $19/month and that is the real price.
- We have AI features (pre-session brief, post-session note draft, Practice Assistant chat). They do not.

## What we do NOT do that competitors do

We are not the right answer for every therapist. Things we do not support:

- **Multi-therapist clinics.** If you have two or more practitioners sharing clients, ClinicSense or Jane App is built for that. We are solo-first.
- **Heavy insurance billing.** If most of your sessions are insurance-billed, Jane App handles it directly. We do not.
- **Class scheduling and packages for groups.** If you teach yoga classes alongside massage, Vagaro has class management. We do not.
- **Inventory management for product retail.** If product sales are 30 percent of your revenue, Vagaro or GlossGenius has that built in. We do not.

## How to switch

We can help you migrate. Three steps:

1. Export your client list from your old platform as a CSV.
2. Upload it to MyBodyMap (Clients, Import).
3. Send a "we moved" email to your clients with the new booking page link.

Your client relationships are yours. They follow you. Your historical session notes can be exported from most platforms as PDF and stored in MyBodyMap.

## Trial alongside your current setup

You do not have to switch on day one. Many therapists run MyBodyMap alongside their existing platform for a month. Test the booking flow, the intake, the cancellation policy. If it saves you time, switch fully. If not, no harm done.
