You can refund any charge from your dashboard with one tap. No need to log into Stripe or Square.

## How to refund

1. Go to Billing in your dashboard
2. Find the charge in the Purchases panel
3. Click the charge row to open it
4. Click Refund
5. Choose Full refund or Partial refund (enter an amount)
6. Confirm

The refund processes through whichever processor handled the original charge (Stripe or Square). Funds typically arrive in the client's account in three to five business days.

## What gets refunded automatically

If you refund through MyBodyMap, three things happen:

1. The processor returns the money to the client's card
2. The transaction is marked Refunded in your billing dashboard and in the client's session history
3. Your reports automatically subtract the refunded amount from your revenue numbers

You do not have to update anything manually.

## Processor fees on refunds

Stripe and Square handle refunded fees differently:

- **Stripe** returns the original 2.9 percent plus thirty cents to you when you refund within 90 days. After 90 days, the fee is not returned.
- **Square** does not return the fee on refunds. You absorb the fee.

For most refunds in the same week, this difference is pennies. Worth knowing for large refunds or older transactions.

## Partial refunds

You can refund any amount up to the original charge. Common partial refund cases:

- Client used part of a six-pack package, refund the unused portion
- Client cancelled within policy, you charged 50 percent, you decide to refund 25 percent as goodwill
- Refund the session price but keep the deposit

## Disputes (chargebacks)

If a client disputes a charge with their bank instead of asking you for a refund:

1. You get an email and a dashboard alert: "Dispute on transaction X"
2. The disputed amount is held by the processor until resolved
3. You have ten to twenty days to respond with evidence
4. We auto-attach the cancellation policy mandate (signed agreement, IP, timestamp) as evidence if the dispute is over a cancellation charge

Most disputes are won by merchants who have a clean signed mandate. Cancellation policy charges with our audit trail win at high rates. Random disputes (client claims they did not authorize the charge) sometimes lose to bank policy regardless of evidence.

## When to refund versus dispute the dispute

If the client has a real reason and you would have refunded anyway, accept the dispute. Less time, same outcome.

If the client is being unreasonable and your audit trail is clean, contest it. We auto-fill most of the response form.
