Services are the menu of work you offer. This is the first thing to set up after signup.

## Where to add services

Settings, then Services. You will see a list (empty if you are new) and a button to add a new service.

## What goes into a service

Each service has these fields:

- **Name.** What clients see on the booking page. Examples: "Deep tissue 60 min," "Prenatal 90 min," "Lymphatic drainage."
- **Duration.** How long the session takes, including transition time. Sixty, seventy-five, ninety minutes are common.
- **Price.** What you charge. We do not take a cut.
- **Description.** Optional, two to four sentences. What this service is for, who it is right for. Skip if not needed.
- **Required deposit.** Optional. If you require a deposit at booking, set the amount or percentage here.

## How many services to start with

Start with three to six. Whatever you actually do most. You can add more later.

A common starting set:
- Swedish 60 min
- Swedish 90 min
- Deep tissue 60 min
- Deep tissue 90 min
- Couples or specialty (if relevant)

## Pricing tiers within a service

If you charge different prices for different conditions (first-timers, returning clients, longer sessions), make them separate services. Trying to handle pricing tiers inside one service makes the booking page confusing for clients.

## Deactivating a service

You can hide a service without deleting it (keeps your historical session data clean). Edit the service, toggle Active off. The service no longer shows on the booking page but remains in your reports.

## Cycle-aligned scheduling

If you want certain services to only show during certain phases of your menstrual cycle (something only MyBodyMap offers), tag each service with the phases it should appear in. Settings, then Cycle. Clients never see your cycle info; they just see the services available that week.
