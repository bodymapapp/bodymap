Session notes are how you record what happened during a session. MyBodyMap supports both quick notes and full SOAP-format documentation, on every plan including Bronze.

## Quick notes versus SOAP

**Quick notes** are a few sentences after the session. "Worked on right shoulder, deep tension in upper trap, gave stretches to take home." Most therapists use quick notes for most sessions.

**SOAP notes** are the four-section clinical format:
- **Subjective:** what the client reported (pain, history, complaints)
- **Objective:** what you observed (range of motion, tissue quality, posture)
- **Assessment:** your professional read of the situation
- **Plan:** what you did and what comes next

Use SOAP when you need clinical-grade documentation (insurance billing, medical-grade injury work, court cases). Most cash-pay therapists use quick notes.

## How to write notes

After every session, the post-session brief auto-opens with:

- Client name and session details
- The intake info they filled out
- A blank notes field
- A toggle to expand to SOAP format if needed
- Suggested phrases based on the body map (you tap to insert)

You write your notes, hit Save. Done.

If you forget to write notes immediately, the session shows up in your dashboard as "needs notes" until you do. You can write them later from the client's profile.

## AI-drafted notes

If you have the AI features turned on (Silver and Gold tiers), MyBodyMap can draft your notes for you. Tap Draft notes after the session, edit if needed, save. Most therapists use the draft as a starting point and personalize.

The AI draws from:
- The client's intake (focus areas, conditions)
- Your previous notes for this client
- The session length and service type

It cannot make up things you did. If you did not press deep tissue work, the draft will not say you did. It will only suggest things that fit the documented context.

## Privacy of notes

Your session notes are visible only to you. Clients do NOT see them. Other therapists do NOT see them. We never train AI across practices on your notes; your data stays in your practice.

If you ever leave MyBodyMap, you can export all your notes as PDF or CSV from Settings, Export Data.

## SOAP for insurance billing

We do not currently submit insurance claims directly. If you bill insurance, you can:

1. Write SOAP notes in MyBodyMap
2. Export them as PDF
3. Submit through whatever insurance system your billing service uses

Most cash-pay therapists do not need this. If insurance billing is your primary need, Jane App may be a better fit for you.

## Why some competitors paywall this

ClinicSense puts SOAP notes behind their higher tier. We do not. Notes (quick or SOAP) are on every plan. We believe documentation is core to massage therapy practice and should not be a premium add-on.
