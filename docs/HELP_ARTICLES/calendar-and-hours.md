Your booking page only shows times you have available. Setting hours correctly is the difference between getting bookings and getting nothing.

## Setting your weekly hours

Settings, then Availability.

For each day of the week, you can:
- Set hours (start, end), or
- Mark the day off

Common patterns:
- Tuesday through Saturday, ten to six
- Monday, Wednesday, Friday, nine to two
- Different morning and afternoon blocks (e.g. nine to noon, then two to six)

You can have multiple time blocks per day. The booking page shows all of them.

## Buffer between sessions

Settings, Availability, then the Buffer field. We recommend fifteen to thirty minutes between sessions. Gives you time for laundry, drinking water, and being a person.

## Time zones

Set your time zone in Settings, Availability. Important if your clients book from another time zone, which happens.

## Blocking specific dates

For vacations, sick days, or "I am taking the month off":

1. Calendar (in your dashboard, not Settings)
2. Click the date range you want to block
3. Choose Block this date or Block this range
4. Add a note if you want (private, only you see it)

Blocked dates do not show on the booking page. Existing bookings on a date you block do not get cancelled automatically. You handle those manually.

## Cal.com sync

If you use Cal.com (or Google Calendar through Cal.com), MyBodyMap can two-way sync. Settings, Calendar, Connect Cal.com. Existing events on Cal.com block out time on MyBodyMap and vice versa.

If you do not use Cal.com, no need to set anything up. Most therapists do not need calendar sync because they only manage their schedule in MyBodyMap.

## Recurring blocks

For things like "every Friday at noon I have lunch with my mom for an hour," create a recurring block. Calendar, click the time, choose Recurring.

## What clients see

Your booking page shows only available time slots. They cannot see your blocked time or your buffer logic. They just see what is open.
