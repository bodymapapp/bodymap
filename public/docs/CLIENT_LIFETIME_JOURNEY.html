The Client Lifetime Journey

Every notification MyBodyMap sends, plotted in time, from a client's first contact to their last. A retention engine disguised as a communication strategy.

Read this whole document as a story arc: one client, one therapist, the relationship from first hello to lifelong return. Every notification marker is a question we asked: at this moment, does the platform say something that strengthens the relationship, or does it stay quiet?

The framing principle

Notifications are not a feature. They are the platform's primary instrument for keeping a client in the practice for years instead of months. Every message has a job to do at a specific moment in the relationship.

Some are nice, some are frustrating. The frustrating ones stay on by default when the data shows they pay off in retention. We document the math for each one so nobody has to argue about whether to keep it on. Therapists who object can turn any individual notification off.

Two channels go to two audiences. The therapist gets in-app bell + email + SMS (their choice per event). The client gets email + SMS only (no in-app, because they have no login). Defaults differ by both audience and life stage.

Off-ramps (anytime in the journey)

These aren't a phase, they're moments that can happen at any point. The platform's job: name what happened, attach the policy, keep the door open. This is where most competitors fail badly.

Frictionless. The client feels respected; the therapist feels respected. Both feelings increase rebook rate.

How to maintain this document

Any pull request that touches a booking state transition, a payment, or a client lifecycle moment must update this timeline. If a notification isn't on this page, it doesn't exist.

Every quarter, we review: any notification with >20% opt-out rate goes back to the drawing board on copy and frequency. Any notification with <30% open rate gets a subject-line rewrite or becomes a digest.

The principle stays the same: notifications are the platform's primary instrument for keeping a client in the practice for years. Every message has a job to do at a specific moment. We earn the right to send it by showing what it pays back.
